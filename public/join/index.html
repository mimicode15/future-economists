Explore Technology and Economics With Us!#

We’re looking for high school students who want to research emerging technologies and write about their economic impact.

No experience is required, just a passion for writing, researching, and learning.

What We Do#

We do research on technologies like AI, blockchain, clean energy, and biotech, and write articles explaining their economic impacts.

Things like: “How is AI changing the job market?” “How is the Blockchain challenging traditional banking systems?” “What affect will clean energy have on economic efficiency?”

Why Join?#

Build real skills

  • Research and fact-checking
  • Clear writing and communication
  • Data analysis and critical thinking
  • Meeting deadlines and working remotely

Create a portfolio

  • Published articles with your name
  • Work you can show colleges and scholarships
  • Evidence you can research, write, and follow through

Learn by doing

  • Deep knowledge of the technologies everyone’s talking about
  • Understanding of how tech companies and markets work
  • Insights into career paths and industries

Join a community

  • Other high schoolers interested in tech and economics
  • People to discuss ideas with
  • Potential collaborators and friends!

Stand out

  • Something interesting to talk about in college interviews
  • Demonstrated initiative and expertise
  • Real work, not just clubs or test scores

What We’re Looking For#

Writers & Researchers#

Research a technology or company, write an article explaining the economics of it, cite your sources, revise based on feedback.

Time commitment: 4-8 hours/week for 1-2 articles per month

You’d fit if: You’re curious, can write clearly, and enjoy research

Data Analysts#

Find economic data, create charts and visualizations, help writers back up claims with numbers.

Time commitment: 3-6 hours/week

You’d fit if: You like working with spreadsheets, data, and making sense of numbers

Designers#

Create article headers, infographics, and social media graphics that help visualize concepts.

Time commitment: 3-5 hours/week

You’d fit if: You know Canva, enjoy graphic design, and want portfolio pieces

Social Media#

Post content on Instagram and TikTok, create short-form articles, engage with followers.

Time commitment: 3-5 hours/week

You’d fit if: You understand social platforms and can make content engaging

What You Need#

Required:

  • Genuine interest in tech and/or economics
  • Commitment to contribute regularly (even if just monthly)
  • Willingness to research thoroughly and fact-check
  • Ability to take feedback and improve
  • Reliable communication

Not required:

  • Previous writing experience
  • Economics knowledge
  • Tech expertise
  • Perfect grades

We care more about curiosity and willingness to learn and contribute than credentials.

The Reality#

This takes real work. You’ll spend hours researching, writing, and revising.

You need to be reliable. We depend on each other to produce content consistently. If you commit, we need you to follow through (or reach out early if you can’t).

You’ll get feedback. Your first drafts will need work. We all improve through the editing process!

But it’s worth it. You’ll learn more than you would from most classes, build real skills, and create work that you can be proud of!

How to Apply#

Email: futureeconomists@gmail.com

Include:

1. Who You Are (3-4 sentences)

  • Name, grade, where you’re from
  • What you’re interested in
  • Why tech economics appeals to you

2. What Role Interests You

  • Writer, data, design, or social media?
  • Any relevant skills or experience? (AP Micro/Macroeconomics, computer science knowledge, etc)

3. Show Us Your Thinking

Pick a prompt and write 200-400 words:

Option A: Pick one technology (AI, crypto, EVs, etc.) and explain how it makes money

Option B: What’s one technology you think is overhyped and why?

Option C: How would you explain DeFi(decentralized finance) to someone who knows nothing about crypto?

Option D: What’s one thing about tech or economics that you’re confused about and want to understand better?

4. Logistics

  • How much time can you commit per week?
  • When could you start?
  • Any questions for us?

Optional: Links to any writing, projects, social accounts, etc.

What Next#

  1. We review your email (usually within a week)
  2. Trial project - Write one article or complete one project to see if you like it
  3. Join the team - Get access to our Discord and start contributing!

FAQ#

Do I need to be good at writing? Not yet. You just need to be willing to learn and improve.Everyone gets better with practice and feedback!

What if I don’t know much about economics or tech? No problem! You’ll research and learn. Sometimes, the best writers are people learning the topic themselves.

How much time really? Minimum 3-4 hours/week(or more if you want to be more active) Be honest about how much time you can allocate efficiently.

Will this help with college apps? Yes. Published research writing demonstrates initiative, expertise, and follow-through. Plus you’ll have interesting stuff to discuss in interviews!

Can I quit if it’s too much? Yes. Just let us know in advance.

Do you accept freshmen? Yes! We care about ability and commitment, not grade level.

Can I contribute without joining the team full-time? We prioritize regular team members, but we occasionally accept guest articles, so please mention this in your email.

What We Expect#

From you:

  • Research thoroughly and cite sources
  • Write clearly and revise based on feedback
  • Meet deadlines (or communicate early if you can’t)
  • Engage professionally
  • Fact-check everything

From us:

  • Clear feedback on your work
  • Support when you’re learning
  • Recognition for your contributions
  • Honest communication
  • A supportive team!
